Hunger Cry
Rhythmic · Repetitive · Escalates

Starts soft and rhythmic — a steady "wah-wah-wah." Builds in intensity if not responded to. Pauses briefly as if waiting, then restarts louder.

RootingFists to mouthPauses & restarts
😴
Tired Cry
Whiny · Nasal · Fades in & out

Whiny, almost nasal quality. Fades in and out — more fussing than full crying. Sounds almost like a complaint. Yawning or eye-rubbing between cries.

YawningEye rubbingFading rhythm
😣
Discomfort / Gas
Urgent · Strained · Sudden bursts

Comes in sudden bursts — loud, urgent, then briefly quiet. Sounds strained, like the baby is pushing. Stops and starts unpredictably. Not soothed by feeding.

Legs curled upArched backStops & starts
🙈
Overstimulated
Escalating · Peaks · Inconsolable

Builds steadily to a frantic peak. Nothing seems to help in the moment. Baby turns head away or stiffens when you try to soothe. Quieter environment is the fix.

Turns awayStiff bodyEscalates fast
💢
Pain Cry
High-pitched · Sudden · Piercing

Sudden, high-pitched, piercing. Often starts with a sharp intake of breath, then a sustained intense cry. No rhythm. Doesn't follow a pattern. Trust your gut — this one feels different.

Questions

Frequently asked

Does this work for newborns, or is it for older babies?

+

It works for both! The guide includes age-by-age breakdowns from newborn through 6 months. Each stage has its own cry patterns and body language cues, and the guide walks you through each one clearly.

Is this based on the Dunstan Baby Language method?

+

No. This guide does not use any proprietary cry-sound systems. Instead, it focuses entirely on observable behaviors — body language, facial cues, timing, and context. No need to train your ear to specific sounds.

+

Every baby is different, and no guide can promise a magic fix. What this guide does is give you a systematic framework to observe and respond — which significantly reduces the guesswork. Most parents notice a real difference within the first few days of using it.
